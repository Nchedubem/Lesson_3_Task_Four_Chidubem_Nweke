TACKLING THE CLIMATE CRISIS

Social change is a common feature of social reality that widely described and explained the evolution of human societies as well as the transformation in the basic components of human society right from the traditional to modern-day societies. There are alterations in social values, norms, institutions, relationships and stratification as a result of internal and external forces of social change. The external forces are events that occurred on a large scale, affecting an entire nation or several nations altogether such as climate change. While internal forces are events that occurred on a small scale causing internal structural rearrangements with greater influences on the social fabric of the society, i.e. demographic change.

Definition of Social Change

Social change is defined as the shift within the social structure, characterized by changes in cultural symbols, rules of behaviour, social organization or value systems. Social change can also be defined as any major modification in the basic structure of the society or social institution that have a huge influence on family life, politics, economy, religion, education, etc.

Positive and Negative Social Change

Social change can be either positive or negative. Positive social change implies the desired development and improvement in the entire or some parts of societal institutions which include culture, economy, education, family, international affairs, law, population, politics and religion, which invariably enhance human and social conditions. While a negative social change shows the dysfunction in the entire or some parts of social structures and institutions which in turn destabilize human and social development.

Social change has significant impact on social, economic, environmental and political conditions. 1. The social impact of social change is the shift of the social elements (like the norms, values, culture, and behavioural patterns) and social arrangements like the (roles, status, structure and institution). 2. The economic impact of social change is on the process of industrialization, commerce, trade formation, as well as work, human and material administration. 3. The political impact of social change influences the political organization and administration of power, resources and governance. 4. The environmental impact of social change is the alteration of the way natural and mineral resources are being managed and utilized by the people.

Some social changes in Nigeria

1. Poverty. 60% of residents in Nigeria live in poverty, lacking access to sufficient resources such as healthcare, schooling, and income. With more of the population in poverty, there is lower standard of living among the population. This is also due to high unemployment, with over 25% of Nigerians without a job. The government is struggling to create new jobs and investments, leading to higher inequality within the country.

2. Inequality in share of dividends of democracy. Inequality in terms of access to social amenities.

3. Terrorism. The Boko Haram terrorist group in Nigeria is strictly against western culture, including voting, religion and secular education. Their means of fighting against these cultural differences is by kidnapping, attacking, and killing people, communities, and different religious groups

How to create social change in Nigeria

1. Technology plays a very key role in driving the growth and development of the society. Establishment of tech hubs and tech startups will solve social change to a great extent.

2. Establish an organization that is targeted to creating social change among a particular group of individuals.

3. Establish a business that is targeted to offering products and service that will create a positive change in the society.

4. Green life projects such as planting of trees, sensitization on adequate waste disposal and maximum use of wastes for biological purposes, etc
